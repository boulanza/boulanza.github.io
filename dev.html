Consumers Energy

Applications Developer Intern (May 2014 - Aug 2014),
Web Design Intern (June 2013 - Aug 2013)

During my first summer with Consumers Energy, I worked for the Workforce Engagement and Strategy Team, where I helped them launch a new web-based learning system on the internal websites of different teams across the company. I created and updated these websites using Microsoft Sharepoint, HTML, CSS, and Javascript. I also re-designed other parts of the websites as a whole in order to make them easier to use and to draw attention to the new features I was adding, as well as the older but still widely used ones.

During my second summer with them, I worked on the Applications Development team. There, I programmed primarily in .NET (using C#, ASP.NET, Model-View-Controller Patterns, etc) and SAP (using ABAP). I often pair-programmed with other developers and helped work on anything from the SAP backend to the .NET front end of their various applications. An example of a project I worked on while I was there was an automatic export of project cards from their Leankit Kanban board, which helped their Agile process become more efficient. This program would run on a server and automatically pull the data from the online board and then upload it to their internal sharepoint website. This freed up space on the site, helped it run quicker, and also freed up the time of the managers who previously had to do this work by hand.

Alliance for the Arts in Research Universities

Student Conference Advisor, Attendee (2013 - 2015)

The Alliance for the Arts in Research Universities (A2RU) is a new national organization that focuses on the place and importance of the arts in academics, technology, research, and community. In 2013 they launched their Emerging Creatives conference for students. I, along with one other student, was chosen to represent students and help plan this conference. I then attended it that year as well as in 2014 and helped out with planning/setup again in 2015. During the conference, students would identify a problem and then work on developing a solution during the weekend-long event. My first year, my group made an app that would ask you questions about what you did during the day and then give you the chance you had of dying that day by measuring the "danger" of said activities. The message of it was supposed to be for you to live life to the fullest since you never know what could happen. The second year my group aimed to revamp the education system. We developed a program for high schools that would connect local business, students, and community leaders together to help foster increased community activity and help keep students engaged.
