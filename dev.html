Projects

Complex Search Visualization

As a part of the Multidisciplinary Design Program, I am part of a student team working with the company ProQuest to design and implement a Web Visualization Tool for Complex Searches. Pharmaceutical companies use ProQuest Dialog to support regulation compliance. Professionals at these companies form very complicated search strategies that can be pages long in order to do a needle-in-a-haystack type search on medical databases.

The purpose of this project is to build a visualization tool/user interface that improves the efficiency of the search process resulting in easier user implementation of correct searches and faster searches that utilize less search engine resources. We are designing, implementing, testing, and planning for the roll-out of this tool. On the front-end, we are using javascript and libraries like D3 in order to visualize these complex, nested search queries and subqueries. The backend uses Java in order to process and "score" these queries, to better help the user understand them. This is a year-long project that I am doing as my senior design project.

ArtsEngine Website Redesign

Another project I'd like to highlight on this page is my redesign of the ArtsEngine website. ArtsEngine is an organization at the University of Michigan that fosters and champions the collaborative creative production of students and faculty through making, instruction, research, community, and the arts. I was hired into their organization as a student web designer after I did some web design work for a child organization of their's, Living Arts.

This was a great experience because, while I was still the primary mind behind the redesign and development work like I was for my own personal website, I now had a boss and coworkers who had ideas and feedback of their own. I followed a similar process to the successful one I found above (as these were happening largely in tandem), albeit with some minor alterations. First, I worked with my boss to figure out exactly what she wanted in the redesign, what she liked and didn't like, what content she wanted to have on it, etc. I then followed this up with pencil-and-paper sketches to get a rough idea before moving on to photoshop mockups. Then, I implemented a basic test version using HTML/CSS that included the home page and example content pages before finally moving into the implementation phase. I then was able to move all of the content from the old site to the new one as well as add the new content, just in time for our launch at the beginning of September before school started! It was a very exciting process and I look forward to continuing to develop and refine their website throughout the coming school year.
